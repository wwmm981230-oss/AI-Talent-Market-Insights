# AI Talent Market Insights

> Notes on AI hiring, talent flow, candidate decisions, and recruiting practice, based on my work in AI recruiting and 300+ conversations with candidates and industry professionals.

## 1. Why I Built This

Over the past few months, I have worked on recruiting projects related to AI, robotics, autonomous driving, and AI infrastructure.

During this process, I spoke with candidates from different backgrounds: big tech, autonomous driving companies, embodied AI startups, robotics teams, cloud vendors, research labs, and hardware-related teams.

I gradually realized that many discussions around AI focus on models, products, financing, and benchmarks, while fewer people talk about the talent side:

* What roles companies are actually hiring for;
* Which candidates are difficult to find;
* How talent moves between big tech, startups, autonomous driving, and robotics companies;
* Why some candidates are open to new opportunities while others hesitate;
* How recruiters can build a more accurate talent map instead of relying only on keywords.

This repository is where I organize these observations.

It is not a complete research report. It is a working notebook that I will continue to update as I learn more.

## 6. Example Observation

A VLA algorithm role is often not just a “model algorithm” role.

In real recruiting work, this kind of role may involve several layers of judgment:

* Whether the candidate has worked on real robotics scenarios;
* Whether the project involves vision-language-action modeling or only general multimodal learning;
* Whether the experience is research-oriented or deployment-oriented;
* Whether the candidate has worked with hardware, sensors, robot platforms, or simulation environments;
* Whether the project experience is deep enough or only mentioned briefly on the resume;
* Whether the candidate’s current company and team are close to the target talent pool;
* Whether the timing, motivation, and location fit the opportunity.

This is why AI technical recruiting cannot stop at keyword matching.

A recruiter still needs to understand the role, the company, the talent pool, and the candidate’s real decision factors.
